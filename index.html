From personal emails to corporate databases, our digital lives are constantly under threat from hackers, scammers, and malicious software. The good news is you do not need to be a tech expert to protect yourself. By following a few key cyber security best practices, you can dramatically reduce your risk of becoming a victim.

1. Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Your password is the first line of defence against cyber attacks. Weak or reused passwords make it easy for attackers to gain access to your accounts.

Best practices:

  • Use at least 12 to 16 characters with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Avoid personal information such as birthdays or pet names.
  • Never reuse the same password across multiple sites.
  • Consider using a password manager to generate and securely store your passwords.

2. Enable Multi Factor Authentication (MFA)

Even if someone steals your password, multi factor authentication adds another layer of security, such as a code sent to your phone or generated by an app. Enable MFA on all accounts that offer it, especially email, banking, and social media platforms.

3. Keep Software and Systems Updated

Cyber criminals exploit outdated software to gain access to your devices.

Best practices:

  • Turn on automatic updates for your operating system, browsers, and apps.
  • Update your antivirus and firewall software regularly.
  • Replace unsupported software and hardware that no longer receive security updates.

4. Be Wary of Phishing Attacks

Phishing remains one of the most common ways hackers steal information. These scams often arrive via email or text, pretending to be from trusted sources.

How to protect yourself:

  • Do not click on suspicious links or attachments.
  • Verify the sender‚Äôs email address carefully.
  • Look for red flags such as spelling errors, urgent requests, or unexpected messages.
  • When in doubt, contact the organisation directly through their official website.

5. Secure Your Wi Fi Network

Your home Wi Fi can be an entry point for cyber criminals.

Tips for Wi Fi security:

  • Change the default router password immediately.
  • Use WPA3 encryption if available.
  • Hide your network SSID and avoid public Wi Fi for sensitive transactions.
  • Consider using a VPN, Virtual Private Network, when on public networks.

6. Back Up Your Data Regularly

Ransomware and hardware failures can destroy your data in seconds.

Protect your files by:

  • Scheduling automatic backups to the cloud or an external hard drive.
  • Keeping at least one backup offline or offsite.
  • Regularly testing backups to ensure they can be restored.

7. Practise Safe Browsing Habits

The internet is full of malicious websites and downloads.

Smart browsing tips:

  • Only download software from trusted sources.
  • Look for HTTPS in the address bar before entering sensitive information.
  • Avoid clicking pop ups or ads that seem too good to be true.

8. Educate Yourself and Your Team

Human error is the weakest link in cyber security.

What you can do:

  • Stay informed about the latest cyber threats.
  • Train students and staff to recognise phishing and social engineering attacks.
  • Foster a culture of cyber awareness; security is everyone‚Äôs responsibility.

Final Thoughts

Cyber security is not a one time task; it is an ongoing process. By following these best practices, you can significantly improve your online safety and resilience against threats. Remember: awareness, consistency, and vigilance are the cornerstones of good cyber security.

Stay safe, stay smart, and protect what matters most: your data.
